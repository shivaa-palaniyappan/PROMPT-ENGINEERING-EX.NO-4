**Experiential Perspective Prompt**

PROMPT:

“Imagine you are a data analyst working for a company. Describe your experience using social media sentiment analysis to understand customer feedback and explain the challenges you face and how you solve them.”

OUTPUT:

As a data analyst in a company, I use social media sentiment analysis to understand how customers feel about our products and services. I collect data from platforms like Twitter and Instagram, clean the data, and apply machine learning models to classify feedback as positive, negative, or neutral. This helps the company quickly identify customer satisfaction levels and improve products.

One major challenge I face is handling sarcasm, slang, and mixed sentiments in social media posts. To solve this, I use advanced models like deep learning and regularly update the training data to improve accuracy. I also filter irrelevant data and continuously monitor model performance to ensure reliable results.

PURPOSE:

The purpose of this experiential perspective prompt is to help the learner understand sentiment analysis from a real-world, practical viewpoint.
It encourages critical thinking by asking the student to apply theoretical knowledge to practical challenges and solutions.

**Everyday Functioning Prompt**

PROMPT:

“Explain how sentiment analysis is used in everyday social media platforms to recommend posts, filter comments, and improve user experience.”

OUTPUT:

Sentiment analysis is used in everyday social media platforms to understand users’ emotions and reactions to posts, comments, and messages. By analyzing whether content is positive, negative, or neutral, platforms can recommend posts that match a user’s interests and preferences, increasing engagement.

It is also used to automatically filter harmful, abusive, or negative comments to maintain a safe online environment. Additionally, companies analyze user feedback through sentiment analysis to improve features, fix issues, and enhance overall user experience.

PURPOSE:

The purpose of this everyday functioning prompt is to show how sentiment analysis is applied in real-life social media platforms.

It helps learners connect theoretical concepts with practical, everyday applications to better understand its importance and impact.

**Prompt Refinement**
Basic Prompt:

Refined Prompt:
“Explain social media sentiment analysis in simple language for undergraduate students. Include its working process, applications, advantages, and challenges. Present the answer in bullet points and limit it to 200 words.”

OUTPUT:
   Social Media Sentiment Analysis is the process of identifying emotions or opinions in social media posts, comments, and reviews.

   It determines whether the text expresses positive, negative, or neutral feelings.

Working Process:

 * Collect data from social media platforms.

 *  Clean the data by removing emojis, links, and unnecessary words.

 *  Apply Natural Language Processing (NLP) techniques.

 *  Use lexicon-based or machine learning models to classify sentiment.

 * Analyze results to understand overall public opinion.

Applications:

 *  Brand monitoring and reputation management.

 * Customer feedback analysis.

 * Market research and product improvement.

 * Political and public opinion analysis.

Advantages:

  *  Provides quick insights from large volumes of data.

  * Helps organizations make data-driven decisions.

  * Improves customer satisfaction and engagement.

  * Difficulty in detecting sarcasm and slang.

  * Handling mixed or unclear sentiments.

  * Requires large and clean datasets for better accuracy.
